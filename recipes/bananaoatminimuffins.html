Banana Oat Mini Muffins

Description

My Sunday morning ritual consists of coffee and a science experiment. I make a batch of these muffins every weekend. Starting with the same base recipe I test various add-ins and toppings that change the flavour and nutrition profile of these mini muffins. Peanut butter powder for extra protein and fewer calories, dry milk powder for tenderness, bran for fiber, toasted bran for fiber and flavour. Don't worry about exact measurements, use what you have and don't be afraid to make substitutions. Just remember to be a conscientious scientist and return here to share your results, successes and failures, we can all learn a thing or two from each other.

I love that this recipe uses normal oatmeal ingredients in muffin form. This is not a wheat muffin with a hint of oatmeal added for texture. This is your breakfast oatmeal, minus the liquid, plus baking powder, blended and baked in a muffin tray. I keep these muffins in the freezer for quick breakfasts and healthy snacks throughout the week.

Ingredients

  • 3 large bananas (very ripe)
  • 2 cups old fashioned oats
  • 1 cup greek yogurt
  • 2 eggs
  • 1-2 tbsps honey (more or less depending on how ripe/sweet your bananas are)
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla
  • 1 tbsp peanut butter (or any nut butter)
  • some cinnamon
  • 2 tsps baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • toppings (optional): chocolate chips, bluberries, dried fruit, etc.

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400Â°F
  2. Lightly oil a mini muffin tray (or any baking tray, be creative)
  3. Add all the ingredients (except toppings) into a blender
  4. Blend until a batter forms, oats should no longer be visible
  5. Pour into muffin tray
  6. Bake for 11-13 minutes or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean (may take longer if topping with frozen blueberries or using a larger baking tray ex. large muffin tray or cake pan)
  7. Storage: Store in the freezer and defrost at room tempurature or in the toaster oven/microwave
