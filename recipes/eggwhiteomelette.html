Egg White Omelette

Description

I make this omelette every day for lunch. Usually, I fill it with veggies and a little bit of crumbled goat cheese. Sometimes I'll form it into a round patty shape and have it as a breakfast sandwich between a two slices of a bagel or wrapped in a tortilla with hot sauce as a breakfast burrito.

Use leftover ingredients as filling for this omelette and report back here when you discover a really great (or really gross) combo.

Ingredients

  • 150g egg whites (around 4 large eggs or 1/2 cup)
  • 1 tsp oil (for oiling pan)
  • salt
  • pepper
  • garlic powder
  • soft goat cheese
  • avocado
  • spinach (fresh or frozen)
  • tomato (diced)
  • caramelized onions

Instructions~

  1. Heat a nonstick skillet over medium high heat
  2. Add oil to the pan and use a spatula to spread it all over the bottom and sides
  3. Pour egg whites into the pan, lower the heat to medium
  4. Sprinkle salt, pepper, and garlic powder over the egg whites
  5. Scatter all the filling ingredients over the egg whites (goat cheese, avocado, tomato, spinach, caramelized onions)
  6. Cover the pan with a lid and let cook until the egg white is no longer runny
  7. Use a spatula to fold the omelette in half and gently press down on the omelette to release any excess liquid from the vegtables or uncooked egg
  8. Continue to press on the omelette until there is no more liquid being released
  9. Plate and serve
